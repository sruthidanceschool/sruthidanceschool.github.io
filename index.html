The Art of Bharatanatyam

Bharatanatyam is one of India's oldest and most revered classical dance forms, originating from the temples of Tamil Nadu. This divine art form is characterized by its emphasis on rapid foot movements, intricate hand gestures (mudras), and expressive facial expressions that work together to tell meaningful stories.

What makes Bharatanatyam truly unique is its ability to encapsulate and portray religious stories, mythological tales, and spiritual themes through precise choreography. Each movement has significance, each gesture tells a story, and each expression conveys deep emotion.

At Sruthi School of Arts, we preserve this ancient tradition while making it accessible to students of all backgrounds, fostering discipline, cultural appreciation, and artistic excellence.

Key Elements

  • Intricate hand gestures (Mudras)
  • Rapid, rhythmic foot movements
  • Expressive facial expressions (Abhinaya)
  • Spiritual storytelling through dance
  • Traditional music and rhythm
